# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Everyday Kenyans: university students streaming between lectures, boutique owners running WhatsApp commerce, families watching football on weekends, remote workers needing reliable connections. They connect from phones and laptops at shared hotspots in estates, campuses, and market areas. Price-sensitive but quality-conscious; they want internet that works without calling a technician.

## Product Purpose
Dice Ltd. sells WiFi hotspot subscriptions. Users connect to a Dice hotspot, a captive portal opens in-browser, they pick a plan (from KSh 10/hour to KSh 1,500/month), pay via M-Pesa, and browse immediately. No app download, no SIM swap, no installation. Success looks like: a visitor lands on the page and within 5 seconds understands "this is where I buy WiFi" and knows the price.

## Brand Personality
Accessible, Reliable, Local. Dice speaks like a sharp neighbor who knows tech, not a corporate entity. Direct language, no jargon. Confident but never flashy. The brand earns trust through clarity and consistency, not through hype.

## Anti-references
- Corporate telecoms (Safaricom/Airtel): cluttered hero banners, promo overload, enterprise-speak, overwhelming nav menus
- Generic SaaS templates: gradient blobs, abstract illustrations, "supercharge your workflow" copy, cookie-cutter startup aesthetic
- Budget ISPs: low-effort designs that undermine trust, clip-art imagery, walls of text, no visual hierarchy

## Design Principles
1. **Clarity on arrival**: A first-time visitor knows what Dice sells within 5 seconds. No abstraction, no metaphor that requires interpretation.
2. **Show the product**: The captive portal IS the product. Show it. Real screens, real flows, real prices.
3. **Earned simplicity**: Every element pulls its weight. Fewer sections done well beat many sections done adequately.
4. **Local credibility**: Prices in KSh, M-Pesa as primary payment, Kenyan names in testimonials. The page should feel like it was made for Nairobi, not localized from a US template.
5. **Speed as proof**: The site itself should be fast. If you sell fast internet, a slow landing page is a contradiction.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Body text contrast 4.5:1 or higher. Reduced-motion alternatives for all GSAP animations. Touch targets 44px minimum for mobile (primary audience). No information conveyed by color alone.
